Who We Serve

Families, students, and job seekers across Western North Carolina — a region where rugged mountain terrain means fiber never came and cellular is spotty at best.

"Helping my community in a meaningful way brings me joy. We all have a role to play in something greater than ourselves."

— Yvette Brooks, Founder & Executive Director
Why It Matters

Without a device or connection, a child can't do homework. A parent can't apply for jobs. A student can't earn a degree online.

The digital divide isn't just an inconvenience — it's a barrier to the life people deserve. Through the Trees turns this problem into a solution, one refurbished device at a time.

Programs

How we close the gap

01

Device Request Program

We provide free refurbished laptops, phones, and tablets to households that qualify. All members in a qualifying household are eligible to receive a device — because one is rarely enough.

Devices are refurbished by community volunteers, tested, and distributed at no cost. We prioritize school and work use cases.

Request a Device →
02

Wifi Hotspot Access

Having a device isn't enough if there's no signal. We provide wifi hotspots to households in rural Appalachia where broadband infrastructure simply doesn't reach.

We address the full problem: access, affordability, and digital literacy.

03

Resale Shop

Our shop at 120 W Main St in downtown Brevard sells affordable refurbished and used tech. Every sale funds the free device program — circular and sustainable by design.

Our Story

Started in a pandemic.
Still going.

When schools closed in March 2020, Yvette Brooks watched children in her community fall behind — not for lack of ability, but lack of connectivity. She started Through the Trees to turn that problem into a solution.

We're still in Brevard, still taking donations, still fixing laptops by hand, and still showing up for our neighbors.
